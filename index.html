The Problem

Hallucination cannot be eliminated.

Three independent formal proofs show that LLM hallucination is mathematically inevitable. Not hard to fix. Impossible.

Learning Theory Proof

Any LLM with a computable ground truth function will inevitably hallucinate when used as a general problem solver. LLMs cannot learn all computable functions.

Xu et al., 2024

Incompleteness Proof

Via Gödel's First Incompleteness Theorem and the Halting Problem. Hallucination stems from the mathematical and logical structure of LLMs themselves.

Banerjee et al., 2024

Quadrilemma Proof

No LLM can simultaneously achieve truthful representation, semantic conservation, complete revelation, and knowledge-constrained optimality.

Karpowicz, 2025

Every AI development workflow treats hallucination as the enemy. RAG. Chain-of-thought. Guardrails. Constitutional AI. Billions of dollars trying to suppress it.

But hallucination is just the AI confidently describing something that doesn't exist yet.

That is also exactly what a product specification does.

If hallucination is inevitable, the productive response is to harness it rather than fight it.

The Solution

Six phases. Fiction to reality.

LUCID converts loose application descriptions into verified software specifications through controlled exploitation of LLM hallucination.

01

Describe

Give the AI a loose, intentionally incomplete description of your application. The gaps are where hallucination does its best work.

02

Hallucinate

The AI writes a Terms of Service as if the app is live in production. It confabulates specific capabilities, SLAs, encryption standards, and data handling -- all in precise legal language.

03

Extract

Every declarative statement becomes a structured, testable requirement. A typical hallucination produces 80-150 extractable claims across security, functionality, performance, and compliance.

04

Build

Implement code to satisfy the extracted requirements. Use any methodology you want -- TDD, agile, vibe coding. The ToS claims are your acceptance criteria.

05

Converge

Verify every claim against the actual codebase. Each gets a verdict: PASS, PARTIAL, FAIL, or N/A. The gap between fiction and reality becomes your backlog.

06

Regenerate

Feed verified reality back. The AI writes an updated ToS that retains what's real and hallucinates new features grounded in what actually exists. Then loop.

How It Works

The Terms of Service trick.

Ask an LLM to write a Terms of Service for an application that doesn't exist. Don't tell it the app doesn't exist. Legal language can't be vague -- so the format forces the model to hallucinate precisely.

Why ToS is the ideal hallucination vehicle

No other document format forces this level of specificity across this many dimensions simultaneously. "The Service may do things" is not a valid legal clause. The format demands precision.
